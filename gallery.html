Due to its extreme environment—high elevation, arid climate, and saline soil—the region hosts very little vegetation. Plant life is scarce across the salt flat itself, but in the surrounding areas, particularly near the edges and in the nearby Eduardo Avaroa Andean Fauna National Reserve, some resilient species can be found.

Among the most notable plants in the region are yareta (Azorella compacta), a dense, moss-like plant that grows very slowly and is often found in rocky, high-altitude terrains. Another common sight is the paja brava, a tough grass that thrives in dry Andean climates. Cacti, such as those on Incahuasi Island in the heart of the salt flat, also add to the unique landscape, standing tall amid the vast white expanse.
